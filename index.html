Mutual Understanding Agreement

Between Akshay Yelsange and Dr. Akshata

Dated · April 28, 2026

℞  Clinically tested for sincerity  ·  No prescription required  ·  Results may vary with caffeine intake

Article I — Introduction

This Agreement is entered into freely, with full cognitive function, minimal prompting, and only a medically acceptable level of butterflies. It is a mutual understanding between Akshay Yelsange (hereinafter "Akshay", "The Party of Considerable Enthusiasm", or "The One Who Googled 'What Does Smitten Mean'") and Dr. Akshata (hereinafter "Dr. Akshata", "The Doctor", or more precisely, "The Leading Clinical Cause of Akshay's Persistently Elevated Heart Rate").

This document carries no legal enforceability — only the quiet weight of genuine intent, a firm handshake of the hearts, and the shared understanding that Wi-Fi passwords will eventually be exchanged.

Noted for the Record
Dr. Akshata once took Akshay's pulse. She never gave it back. This agreement is, in part, an attempt to make that arrangement mutually satisfying.

Article II — Purpose

The purpose of this Accord is to establish a shared framework for a companionship rooted in honesty, mutual respect, and the quiet understanding that personal growth is not a sprint but a rather scenic walk — and both parties agree to enjoy the view, together, without checking Google Maps every five minutes.

More precisely: to codify the terms under which Akshay and Dr. Akshata commit to being consistently excellent to each other — even on days when the rest of the world is aggressively mediocre.

Diagnosis
Prognosis: Excellent. Second opinion: Still excellent. Dr. Akshata is encouraged to note that Akshay's condition is chronic, incurable, and entirely her doing. Treatment: continued presence.

Article III — Key Understandings

  • Dr. Akshata acknowledges that Akshay's dancing, while enthusiastic, is best appreciated as performance art — and agrees that affection shall not be contingent upon rhythm, tempo, or stylistic coherence.
  • Akshay commits to self-improvement — not from obligation, but because stagnation is boring, and this relationship deserves far better than boring.
  • Both parties agree to communicate expectations openly, without passive aggression, cryptic sighing, or the particularly dangerous phrase: "I'm fine."
  • Dr. Akshata reserves the right to diagnose Akshay's moods, but agrees to prescribe conversation rather than silence.
  • Akshay acknowledges that "doing research" and "scrolling reels at 1am" are not the same thing — except on Fridays, by mutual exception.
  • Both recognise that disagreements are symptoms of two people who actually give a damn, and shall be treated accordingly — with patience, not prescription pads.

Article IV — Shared Values & Growth

  • Growth shall be mutual — emotional, intellectual, and occasionally culinary.
  • Dr. Akshata's ambitions are not merely respected; they are actively championed, with the kind of enthusiasm typically reserved for final-year results.
  • Akshay provides emotional support of the unconditional variety — no referral required.
  • Honesty in disagreements is non-negotiable. Winning an argument is far less satisfying than actually understanding each other — both parties accept this reluctantly and completely.
  • Major decisions are made collectively, because two minds — particularly one that cleared med school — are demonstrably better than one.
  • External opinions about this relationship are filed under: Noted, Irrelevant, Archived.
  • Time for each other is not optional; it is the investment that pays compound emotional returns.
  • Dr. Akshata agrees to receive Akshay's humour with clinical tolerance and the occasional genuine, unguarded laugh.
  • Akshay agrees not to self-diagnose via WebMD and spiral — that is explicitly Dr. Akshata's department.
  • Personal space is respected. Individuality is celebrated. Togetherness is chosen, daily.
Side Effects
Known side effects of this relationship include: involuntary smiling at one's phone, an uncharacteristic interest in medicine, the urge to share precisely one meme too many, and a general improvement in one's resting disposition. Consult Dr. Akshata if symptoms persist. She will not be surprised.

Article V — Lifestyle & Shared Experience

Both parties agree to actively invest in shared memories — the most durable form of emotional equity and, notably, the only asset that appreciates with age and laughter.

  • Dinners — unhurried, where the conversation outlasts the dessert and neither party checks the time.
  • Weekend outings, casual or ambitious, guided by the principle that the best stories begin with "remember when we…"
  • Movie nights, with the mutual understanding that commentary during films is a recognised love language in this household.
  • New experiences, including the slightly terrifying ones — which, experience confirms, become the fondest memories.

Akshay commits to thoughtfulness — not grand gestures for an audience, but small, deliberate acts of care that compound, quietly, into something remarkable.

Prescription
℞  One good conversation daily. Two shared laughs, minimum. No ceiling on genuine warmth. Refills: unlimited. Insurance: unnecessary — this one's covered.

Article VI — Duration

This Agreement carries no expiration date, no auto-renewal clause, and no fine print tucked beneath the fold. It is designed to evolve alongside the people who signed it — growing more precise with time, like a diagnosis refined by evidence, and a relationship refined by choice.

Either party may initiate a review at any time — not to renegotiate the spirit of this accord, but to reaffirm it with greater clarity and perhaps better handwriting.

Article VII — Closing Statement

Akshay and Dr. Akshata enter this accord not because relationships require contracts, but because some things deserve to be said — deliberately, clearly, and with enough wit that neither party falls asleep mid-reading.

They recognise that what makes a companionship extraordinary is not the absence of imperfection, but the consistent choice to show up — fully, without disclaimer, and with the kind of warmth that no qualification, however distinguished, can teach and no syllabus can confer.

Final Note
No relationship was harmed in the making of this document. The doctor, however, may need to treat Akshay for an acute, recurrent case of adoration. The prognosis, she will find, is entirely her own doing.
