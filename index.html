The Akshay–Akshata
Accord

A Formally Informal Declaration of Mutual Affection,
Shared Laughter & Occasional Brilliance

Executed this day: April 29, 2026
— ✶ —

This agreement is entered into by and between:

Party of the First Part Akshay Yelsange (hereinafter “Akshay”, “The Defendant”, or “The One Who Started This”)
⟷
Party of the Second Part Dr. Akshata (hereinafter “Dr. Akshata”, “The Honourable Doctor”, or “The CMO of Akshay’s Heart”)

§ I Preamble

WHEREAS Akshay did not require a differential diagnosis to identify his symptoms — elevated heart rate, involuntary smiling, and a sudden, suspicious interest in becoming a better human — all pointing to one undeniable conclusion;

WHEREAS Dr. Akshata possesses both a distinguished medical degree and the rare, formidable ability to cure boredom while simultaneously causing it to reappear in the most delightful way;

WHEREAS both parties acknowledge that this is not a legal contract, a clinical trial, nor a binding prescription — but a sincere, thoughtful, and occasionally absurd declaration of mutual intent;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual admiration, shared wit, and the fact that someone had to put this in writing — both parties agree to the following terms.

§ II Purpose & Intent

The purpose of this accord is to establish a transparent, honest, and admirably over-engineered framework for the companionship between Akshay and Dr. Akshata — one that nurtures growth, embraces imperfection, and never takes itself too seriously.

Both parties enter this agreement not out of obligation, but out of genuine appreciation for the rare and curious phenomenon of finding someone who makes the ordinary feel extraordinary.

§ III Mutual Obligations

  • Both parties commit to communicating with honesty and care — no passive-aggressive silences exceeding 24 hours (extensions subject to mutual negotiation and reasonable cause).
  • Each shall respect the other’s ambitions, career demands, and the sacred right to have a terrible day without it becoming an event requiring formal documentation.
  • “We need to talk” shall never be deployed as an opening statement without reasonable context. Ambiguity is not a form of kindness.
  • Disagreements shall be approached like a sound clinical consultation — with calm inquiry, evidence, and a commitment to resolution rather than point-scoring.
  • Celebrations, both grand and granular, shall be honoured together. Promotions, small wins, and finding a great parking spot all qualify.
  • Past mistakes shall remain sealed. Old case files are not to be re-opened during present disagreements.
  • Personal growth is to be encouraged and celebrated — never weaponised as a measuring stick.

§ IV Akshay’s Commitments

  • Akshay shall plan thoughtful, intentional experiences — not because a lifestyle article suggested it, but because Dr. Akshata is genuinely worth the effort.
  • He commits to showing up — not merely in physical proximity, but with full emotional presence. Attendance is not the same as engagement.
  • Akshay shall listen first and solve second. Not every conversation requires a resolution; sometimes it requires a warm, attentive silence and a well-timed cup of chai.
  • He shall acknowledge, with sincere respect, that Dr. Akshata has survived medical school, clinical rotations, and more sleep deprivation than any human should reasonably endure — and shall never trivialise that resilience.
  • Akshay is hereby obligated to make Dr. Akshata laugh at least once daily. This clause is non-negotiable and classified as a matter of public health.
  • He shall improve consistently — in patience, in presence, and in all the small, deliberate ways that signal to another person they are worth the effort.
  • He shall never trivialise her work, her opinions, her expertise, or her exceptionally well-earned title.

§ V Dr. Akshata’s Commitments

  • Dr. Akshata agrees to extend to Akshay the same calibre of patience she demonstrates with her patients — minus the formal clinical note, hopefully.
  • She commits to accompanying Akshay on outings: dinners, films, walks, and the occasional adventure that does not require prior institutional authorisation.
  • Dr. Akshata agrees not to prescribe “give it time” as the sole treatment for every relational ailment, unless clinically appropriate and mutually agreed upon.
  • She shall receive Akshay’s humour — however experimental the delivery — with at least a baseline level of warmth and a sporting willingness.
  • She commits to allowing herself to be cared for. Brilliance does not preclude the need for tenderness.
  • Dr. Akshata will communicate her needs with clarity. Akshay is perceptive, but not telepathic — and the doctor’s handwriting policy cannot extend to emotional communication.

§ VI Official Medical Disclaimer

In deference to the distinguished credentials of Dr. Akshata, the following clinical observations are hereby formally entered into the record:

  • Diagnosis: Chronic admiration — presenting with recurrent involuntary smiling, elevated resting mood, and a compelling urge to make plans. Prognosis: excellent. No intervention required.
  • Warning: Prolonged exposure to Dr. Akshata may result in irreversible happiness, a heightened appreciation for intelligence, and an inexplicable desire to become a better person. No antidote exists, nor has one been requested.
  • Prescription: One (1) quality date per week, administered with consistency and intention, to maintain optimal relational health. Refills: unlimited. Generic substitutes: not accepted.
  • Contraindication: This accord must not be combined with ego, pettiness, or the assumption that being technically correct is more important than being fundamentally kind.
  • Clinical Note: Akshay’s heart rate has been clinically observed to elevate in Dr. Akshata’s presence. No intervention has been recommended — the arrhythmia appears benign, voluntary, and entirely incurable.
  • Patient Information: Akshay does not require a referral to recognise that Dr. Akshata is precisely who he was looking for. The self-diagnosis was, for once, accurate.
  • Side Effects: Participants enrolled in this accord may experience uncontrollable warmth, an increased appetite for shared experiences, and a persistent inability to stop thinking about the other party. These are features, not adverse events.
  • Second Opinion: Not required. Some things are so obviously right that seeking a second opinion would simply be a waste of perfectly good appointment slots.

§ VII Shared Values & The Architecture of Us

  • Both parties shall grow together — not necessarily at the same pace, but always in the same direction.
  • Unsolicited external opinions on their relationship shall carry the weight of unsolicited medical advice: acknowledged, considered for exactly three seconds, and respectfully set aside.
  • Trust is the foundation. Without it, this accord is simply a very stylish piece of writing on a beautiful background.
  • Both parties agree that love, at its most mature and considered form, is a daily choice — not a passive feeling that waits for ideal circumstances.
  • Vulnerability is not a liability. It is the most sophisticated form of courage — and both parties are expected to practise it with intention.
  • Neither shall permit the busyness of life to become a standing excuse for emotional absence.
  • Time shall be made — not because schedules permit, but because each other is categorically worth prioritising.

§ VIII Duration

This accord carries no expiration date — much like the finest things in life, it is designed to deepen rather than diminish. Both parties may revisit, revise, or enthusiastically reaffirm these terms at any time, ideally over a good meal and better conversation.

§ IX Closing Statement

In drafting this accord, Akshay wishes to convey — with a sincerity that considerably outweighs any comedic clause herein — that Dr. Akshata is someone genuinely remarkable.

Brilliant, composed, and quietly extraordinary in the ways that actually matter. The sort of person for whom a formal document feels not excessive, but entirely appropriate.

“The finest relationships are not between flawless people. They are between imperfect ones who choose, deliberately and repeatedly, to show up for each other.”

Both parties execute this document in good faith, clear minds, and the full awareness that something genuinely worth having is worth the effort of a well-crafted accord.
